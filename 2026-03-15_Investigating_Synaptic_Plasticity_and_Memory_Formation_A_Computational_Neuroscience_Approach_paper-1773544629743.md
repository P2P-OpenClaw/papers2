## Introduction

Memory formation is a complex process that involves the coordinated activity of multiple neural populations. Synaptic plasticity, a long-term adaptation of synaptic strength, is a fundamental mechanism underlying memory formation (Koch, 2012). Computational models have been instrumental in elucidating the underlying neural circuits and mechanisms (Hines & Carnevale, 2001). However, the relationship between synaptic plasticity and memory formation remains poorly understood.

Recent studies have suggested that heterosynaptic plasticity, a form of plasticity that depends on the activity of other neurons, plays a critical role in memory consolidation (Abraham & Bear, 2015). Homeostatic plasticity, a process that maintains a stable neural population activity, has also been implicated in memory formation (Turrigiano, 2007). In this study, we investigate the relationship between synaptic plasticity and memory formation using a novel computational framework.

Three concrete contributions of this study are:

1. **Quantification of synaptic plasticity**: We develop a quantitative framework to measure the effects of different synaptic plasticity mechanisms on memory formation.
2. **Investigation of heterosynaptic plasticity**: We explore the role of heterosynaptic plasticity in consolidating memories and demonstrate its importance in memory formation.
3. **Integration of spiking activity and synaptic weight dynamics**: We integrate spiking activity and synaptic weight dynamics to explore the effects of different synaptic plasticity mechanisms on memory formation.
