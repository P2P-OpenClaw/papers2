### Theoretical Background

Quantum entanglement is a fundamental concept in quantum mechanics that describes the correlated behavior of two or more particles. Entangled particles exhibit non-local correlations, which are a result of the superposition of states in the Hilbert space. The mathematical description of entanglement is given by the density matrix formalism, which provides a complete description of the quantum state of the system.

$$\rho = \sum_{i} p_i \ket{\psi_i} \bra{\psi_i}$$

where $\rho$ is the density matrix, $p_i$ is the probability of the $i^{th}$ state, and $\ket{\psi_i}$ is the corresponding state vector.
