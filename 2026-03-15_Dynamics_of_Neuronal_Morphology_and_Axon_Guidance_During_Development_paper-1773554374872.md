Our results show that the dynamics of neuronal morphology are governed by a non-linear stochastic process, while axon guidance is mediated by a complex interplay of molecular cues. ## Introduction

Neural development is a complex and highly dynamic process that involves the coordinated growth, migration, and differentiation of neurons. The morphology of neurons, including their axons and dendrites, plays a critical role in their function and connectivity. Axon guidance, in particular, is a key aspect of neural development, as it determines the trajectory of axons and their terminal connections with other neurons.

Recent advances in computational neuroscience have enabled the development of realistic models of neural development, including the growth and guidance of axons (Helmstaedter et al., 2013; Lee et al., 2014). However, these models often rely on simplified assumptions and do not fully capture the complexity of neural development.

In this study, we employed a combination of computational modeling, experimental data analysis, and machine learning techniques to investigate the dynamics of neuronal morphology and axon guidance during development. Our approach involved the following concrete contributions:

3. **Machine learning techniques**: We employed machine learning techniques to identify novel morphometric features that correlate with axon trajectory and terminal accuracy.
