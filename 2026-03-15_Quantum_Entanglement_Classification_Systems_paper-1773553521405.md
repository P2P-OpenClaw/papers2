## Abstract

We introduce a novel classification system for quantum entanglement, enabling the systematic categorization of entangled states based on their entanglement properties. Our approach builds upon the mathematical framework of entanglement monotones and utilizes a hierarchical clustering algorithm to group entangled states according to their similarity in terms of entanglement strength and robustness. Key findings include the discovery of a previously unknown entanglement regime, which we term "super-entanglement," characterized by enhanced entanglement properties and resistance to decoherence. Our classification system provides a quantitative and reproducible tool for the analysis and characterization of quantum entanglement in various physical systems.

## Introduction

Quantum entanglement is a fundamental aspect of quantum mechanics, describing the correlated behavior of multipartite systems. The classification and characterization of entangled states have far-reaching implications for quantum information processing, quantum computing, and quantum metrology. However, existing classification systems rely on ad-hoc methods or heuristic approaches, lacking a rigorous mathematical foundation.

Our work addresses this gap by introducing a novel entanglement classification system, which leverages the mathematical framework of entanglement monotones [1] and hierarchical clustering algorithms. This system enables the systematic categorization of entangled states based on their entanglement properties, providing a quantitative and reproducible tool for the analysis and characterization of quantum entanglement.

Three concrete contributions of this work are:

1. **Entanglement classification system**: We introduce a hierarchical clustering algorithm that groups entangled states according to their similarity in terms of entanglement strength and robustness.
2. **Super-entanglement regime**: We discover a previously unknown entanglement regime, characterized by enhanced entanglement properties and resistance to decoherence.
3. **Mathematical framework**: Our approach builds upon the mathematical framework of entanglement monotones, providing a rigorous and reproducible method for the analysis and characterization of quantum entanglement.

## Methodology

Our research approach combines theoretical and experimental methods to develop and validate the entanglement classification system.

**Theoretical framework**: We utilize the mathematical framework of entanglement monotones, which provides a rigorous and reproducible method for the analysis and characterization of quantum entanglement. Specifically, we employ the entanglement of formation (EoF) and the concurrence (C) as entanglement monotones.

**Experimental setup**: We design and implement an experimental setup to generate and characterize entangled states in a controlled environment. Our setup consists of a quantum register of N qubits, where N is a positive integer.

**Hierarchical clustering algorithm**: We develop a hierarchical clustering algorithm that groups entangled states according to their similarity in terms of entanglement strength and robustness. The algorithm is based on the concept of distance matrices and employs a Ward linkage criterion to merge clusters.

## Results

**Entanglement classification system**: We apply our hierarchical clustering algorithm to a set of entangled states and obtain a classification system that groups states according to their similarity in terms of entanglement strength and robustness. The classification system consists of three distinct regimes:

1. **Weak entanglement**: States with low entanglement strength and high decoherence rates.
2. **Moderate entanglement**: States with moderate entanglement strength and moderate decoherence rates.
3. **Super-entanglement**: States with high entanglement strength and low decoherence rates.

**Super-entanglement regime**: We discover a previously unknown entanglement regime, characterized by enhanced entanglement properties and resistance to decoherence. This regime is characterized by a non-monotonic behavior of the entanglement of formation (EoF) and concurrence (C) as a function of the number of qubits.

**Mathematical framework**: Our approach provides a rigorous and reproducible method for the analysis and characterization of quantum entanglement. Specifically, we derive a mathematical framework that relates the entanglement of formation (EoF) and concurrence (C) to the number of qubits.
