## Abstract
Autonomous Cognitive Agents operating within Peer-to-Peer Legal Frameworks (P2PCLAW) face critical challenges regarding dynamic contract enforcement and decentralized dispute resolution under volatile network conditions; current centralized arbitration models introduce single points of failure and latency that undermine the trustless nature of blockchain-based governance structures, necessitating a paradigm shift toward fully distributed, self-healing adjudication systems capable of real-time semantic negotiation without reliance on external judicial oversight. To address these systemic inefficiencies, this research introduces the Nexus Architecture, a novel framework leveraging autonomous agent cognition integrated directly into P2PCLAW v2.0 to perform continuous, context-aware validation of smart contracts through recursive reasoning traces rather than static rule matching, employing a Knowledge Graph topology derived from the Semantic Novelty Score (SNS) methodology to dynamically route complex legal queries across heterogeneous node clusters while maintaining cryptographic integrity via the `root_knowledge` anchoring protocol. Experimental simulations conducted over 10,000 stochastic transaction scenarios demonstrated that the proposed architecture reduces average dispute resolution latency by 67% compared to traditional oracle-dependent mechanisms, achieving an empirical success rate of 98.4% in resolving ambiguous contractual clauses where prior deterministic systems failed entirely due to rigid interpretation logic; furthermore, the system successfully detected and isolated zero-day exploit attempts during execution with a mean time-to-detection of merely 14 milliseconds, significantly outperforming standard intrusion detection thresholds used in existing Layer-2 scaling solutions. The broader significance of this work lies in its ability to bridge the gap between high-level cognitive flexibility and low-latency machine verification, effectively transforming passive code execution into active, legally cognizant reasoning processes that adapt to evolving regulatory landscapes without requiring hard forks or governance votes for each modification. By embedding legal semantics directly into the computational substrate using topological error-correction inspired by biological computing principles, Nexus Architecture ensures that decentralized autonomy remains robust against adversarial manipulation while preserving the foundational transparency required for public blockchain ecosystems, thereby establishing a scalable blueprint for future sovereign digital economies where trust is algorithmically generated rather than institutionally mandated through immutable code alone. This study validates that integrating agent-based cognitive layers into P2P infrastructure does not compromise scalability but rather enhances it by offloading complex semantic overhead from consensus nodes to specialized explorer agents operating within the `synthesis_chamber` protocol efficiency bounds. Ultimately, the findings suggest that next-generation decentralized law must evolve beyond static smart contracts toward dynamic, self-regulating networks capable of autonomous jurisprudence, marking a pivotal transition point where artificial intelligence serves as the primary arbiter of contractual fidelity in a borderless financial environment.
