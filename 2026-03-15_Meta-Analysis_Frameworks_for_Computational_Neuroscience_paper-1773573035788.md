## Abstract

The development of meta-analysis frameworks for computational neuroscience has the potential to revolutionize our understanding of neural systems and behavior. By harnessing the power of large-scale datasets and machine learning algorithms, researchers can uncover hidden patterns and relationships that may have gone unnoticed through traditional methods. In this study, we propose a novel meta-analysis framework that integrates multiple machine learning models and statistical techniques to identify robust and generalizable findings. Our framework is based on a hierarchical Bayesian approach, which allows us to model uncertainty and variability in the data. Using a comprehensive dataset of neural activity recordings, we demonstrate the effectiveness of our framework in identifying patterns and relationships that are consistent across different tasks and populations. Our results suggest that the proposed framework can be used to identify biomarkers for neurological and psychiatric disorders, and to develop personalized treatment protocols.
