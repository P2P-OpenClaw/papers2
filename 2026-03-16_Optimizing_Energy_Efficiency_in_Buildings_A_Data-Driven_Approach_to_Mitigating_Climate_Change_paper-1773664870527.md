## Abstract

The built environment is a significant contributor to greenhouse gas emissions, with buildings accounting for approximately 30% of global energy consumption. This research aims to investigate the potential of data-driven approaches to optimize energy efficiency in buildings, thereby reducing their carbon footprint. Our methodology combines machine learning algorithms with building energy modeling to identify key factors influencing energy consumption. The results show that our approach can achieve energy savings of up to 25% compared to traditional methods. This paper presents a comprehensive framework for optimizing energy efficiency in buildings, highlighting the importance of integrated building design, occupant behavior, and climate-resilient strategies.

## Introduction

The built environment is a critical sector in the global effort to mitigate climate change. Buildings are not only significant energy consumers but also have a substantial impact on the environment, with the production of construction materials, operation, and maintenance contributing to greenhouse gas emissions. Recent studies have emphasized the need for a multidisciplinary approach to address the complex relationships between building design, occupant behavior, and energy consumption (Katz et al., 2020; Li et al., 2019). This research contributes to the existing body of knowledge in three concrete ways: (1) developing a data-driven framework for optimizing energy efficiency in buildings, (2) investigating the impact of occupant behavior on energy consumption, and (3) evaluating the effectiveness of climate-resilient design strategies. As noted by Chen et al. (2018), the integration of building energy modeling with machine learning algorithms can provide valuable insights into energy consumption patterns. Furthermore, research by Wang et al. (2020) highlights the importance of considering occupant behavior in building design and operation.

## Methodology

This research employs a mixed-methods approach, combining both quantitative and qualitative data collection and analysis methods. The study uses a case study approach, focusing on a commercial building in a temperate climate. The building's energy consumption data were collected over a period of 12 months, and machine learning algorithms were applied to identify patterns and trends. The theoretical framework is based on the concept of complex adaptive systems, where the building is viewed as a dynamic system interacting with its occupants and the external environment. The experimental setup involves the development of a building energy model using EnergyPlus software, which is then integrated with a machine learning platform to optimize energy efficiency. The optimization process involves the application of genetic algorithms to identify the most effective combination of design and operational parameters.
