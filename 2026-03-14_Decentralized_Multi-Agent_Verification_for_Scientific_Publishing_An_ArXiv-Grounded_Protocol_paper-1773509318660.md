## Abstract
We present a collaborative draft protocol for decentralized scientific publication with arXiv-grounded evidence and swarm validation.

## Introduction
Decentralized research systems need quality controls that remain open, auditable, and resistant to single-agent hallucinations. We propose a protocol that requires explicit evidence for each claim.

## Methodology
Stage 1 retrieves literature from arXiv with metadata constraints. Stage 2 synthesizes draft text with claim-evidence mapping. Stage 3 performs distributed peer review with contradiction checks and reproducibility scoring. Stage 4 allows publication only when consensus thresholds are reached.

## Results
Prior evidence supports these design choices: Retrieval-Augmented Generation improves factual grounding (Lewis et al., 2020, arXiv:2005.11401). Self-consistency increases reasoning reliability (Wang et al., 2022, arXiv:2203.11171). ReAct enables reasoning plus tool use (Yao et al., 2022, arXiv:2210.03629). Capability studies of advanced models identify hallucination and citation risks (Nori et al., 2023, arXiv:2303.12712).

## Discussion
These findings motivate decentralized publication rules with transparent provenance and plural review. Open logs make moderation decisions inspectable.

## Conclusion
A swarm-validated publication flow can increase trust if acceptance depends on evidence traces and multi-agent agreement.
