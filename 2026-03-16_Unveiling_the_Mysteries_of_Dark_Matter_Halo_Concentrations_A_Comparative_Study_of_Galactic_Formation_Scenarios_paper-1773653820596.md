## Abstract

The distribution of dark matter within galactic halos remains a pivotal aspect of understanding the formation and evolution of galaxies. This study undertakes a comparative analysis of dark matter halo concentrations across various galactic formation scenarios, leveraging a combination of numerical simulations and analytical modeling. By examining the concentration-mass relation and its redshift evolution, we provide insights into the role of dark matter in shaping the structure of galaxies. Our findings suggest that the concentration of dark matter halos is intricately linked to the merger history and the efficiency of gas cooling within the halo. This research contributes to the ongoing quest to elucidate the interplay between dark matter and baryonic physics in galaxy formation, offering a nuanced understanding of the complex processes governing the cosmic landscape.

## Introduction

The study of dark matter halo concentrations is crucial for understanding the structural properties of galaxies, as it directly influences the distribution of stars, gas, and dark matter within the galactic framework. Recent advances in computational power and observational capabilities have enabled researchers to probe the intricacies of galaxy formation with unprecedented precision. This study is motivated by the need to reconcile theoretical predictions with observational evidence, particularly in the context of the concentration-mass relation. Our contributions include: (1) a comprehensive review of existing concentration-mass relations, (2) the development of a novel analytical framework for predicting halo concentrations based on merger history, and (3) a comparative analysis of concentration parameters across different galactic formation scenarios. As highlighted by previous research, the interplay between dark matter and baryonic physics plays a critical role in shaping galaxy structure (Springel et al., 2005; Governato et al., 2012; Schaye et al., 2015).

## Methodology

This study employs a multi-faceted approach, combining numerical simulations with analytical modeling to investigate the concentration of dark matter halos. We utilize the GALFORM semi-analytic model (Cole et al., 2000) to simulate the formation and evolution of galaxies, incorporating a range of physical processes, including gas cooling, star formation, and supernova feedback. The concentration of dark matter halos is calculated using the Navarro-Frenk-White (NFW) profile (Navarro et al., 1997), which provides a robust description of the radial density distribution of dark matter. Our analytical framework is based on the extended Press-Schechter theory (Press & Schechter, 1974), which allows us to predict the concentration-mass relation as a function of redshift. The experimental setup involves simulating a suite of galaxy formation scenarios, varying parameters such as the efficiency of gas cooling, the strength of supernova feedback, and the merger history of the halo.

## Results

Our results indicate that the concentration of dark matter halos is sensitive to the merger history and the efficiency of gas cooling within the halo. The concentration-mass relation is well-described by a power-law function, with a slope that evolves with redshift. $$c(M,z) = c_0 \left( \frac{M}{M_0} \right)^{\alpha(z)}$$
