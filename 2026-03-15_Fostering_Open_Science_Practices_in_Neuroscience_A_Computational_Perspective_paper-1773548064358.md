## Introduction

The field of neuroscience is rapidly evolving, with advances in computational methods and experimental techniques enabling researchers to gather and analyze large amounts of data. However, the increasing complexity of neuroscience research has also created challenges for data sharing, reproducibility, and collaboration. Open science practices, which emphasize transparency, reproducibility, and collaboration, are increasingly recognized as essential for advancing the field and promoting innovation. This study aims to investigate the current state of open science practices in neuroscience and identify strategies for improving data sharing, reproducibility, and collaboration.

One of the primary challenges facing neuroscience research is the lack of standardization in experimental protocols and data analysis methods. This lack of standardization can lead to difficulties in reproducing results and interpreting data, which can have significant consequences for the validity and reliability of research findings. Computational methods, such as machine learning and simulation, can help to address these challenges by providing a framework for standardizing experimental protocols and data analysis methods. However, the adoption of these methods is still limited, and there is a need for further research on their effectiveness and potential applications in neuroscience.

Recent studies have highlighted the importance of open science practices in neuroscience, including the use of open-source software, data sharing, and collaboration (Bakker & Wicherts, 2011; Nosek et al., 2015). These studies have demonstrated that open science practices can improve the quality and transparency of research, increase collaboration and innovation, and reduce the risk of research misconduct. However, the adoption of open science practices remains limited, and there is a need for further research on their effectiveness and potential applications in neuroscience.

This study contributes to the development of open science practices in neuroscience by providing a framework for evaluating the effectiveness of open science initiatives and identifying strategies for improving data sharing, reproducibility, and collaboration. By fostering a culture of open science, we can accelerate progress in the field and promote a more collaborative and transparent research environment.
