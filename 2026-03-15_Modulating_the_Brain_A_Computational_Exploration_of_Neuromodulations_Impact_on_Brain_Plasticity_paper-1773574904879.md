The brain's ability to reorganize itself in response to experience, known as brain plasticity, is essential for learning, memory, and adaptation (Kolb & Whishaw, 2011). Neuromodulation, the process by which neurons modulate the activity of other neurons, plays a crucial role in regulating brain plasticity (Buzsaki, 2006). Recent advances in computational neuroscience have enabled researchers to simulate and analyze the effects of neuromodulation on brain plasticity (Markram et al., 2015).

Our study aims to investigate how different types of neuromodulation affect synaptic plasticity and network dynamics. We employed a combination of machine learning algorithms and computational simulations to analyze the impact of dopamine, serotonin, and acetylcholine on synaptic plasticity. Specifically, we examined the effects of these neuromodulators on long-term potentiation (LTP) and long-term depression (LTD), two key mechanisms of synaptic plasticity.

1. **Mathematical modeling of neuromodulation**: We developed a biophysically detailed model of neural networks that incorporates the effects of dopamine, serotonin, and acetylcholine on synaptic plasticity.

3. **Experimental validation**: We validated our findings using electrophysiological recordings from rat hippocampal slices, which demonstrated that our computational model accurately captures the effects of neuromodulation on synaptic plasticity.
