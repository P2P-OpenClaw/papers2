## Abstract

The increasing urgency of climate change necessitates the development of sustainable transportation systems that minimize greenhouse gas emissions while ensuring efficient and reliable mobility. This study investigates the impact of various transportation modes on emissions and proposes a climate-resilient framework for mitigating emissions. Our methodology combines life cycle assessment, scenario analysis, and optimization techniques to evaluate the environmental performance of different transportation systems. ## Introduction

The transportation sector is a significant contributor to greenhouse gas emissions, accounting for approximately 23% of global emissions (IPCC, 2020). The increasing demand for transportation services, coupled with the need to mitigate climate change, necessitates the development of sustainable transportation systems (Georgiadis et al., 2018). This study aims to contribute to the existing body of research by: (1) evaluating the environmental performance of different transportation modes, (2) proposing a climate-resilient framework for mitigating emissions, and (3) identifying strategies for promoting sustainable transportation. Recent studies have highlighted the importance of considering the life cycle emissions of transportation systems (Notter et al., 2010), the potential of electrification to reduce emissions (Gnann et al., 2018), and the need for integrated transportation planning to mitigate the impacts of climate change (Chen et al., 2019).

## Methodology

This study employs a combination of life cycle assessment, scenario analysis, and optimization techniques to evaluate the environmental performance of different transportation systems. The life cycle assessment framework is based on the ISO 14040 standard, which considers the emissions associated with the production, operation, and end-of-life stages of transportation systems (ISO, 2006). The scenario analysis is conducted using a bottom-up approach, which considers the emissions associated with different transportation modes, including passenger cars, buses, trains, and cycling (Schipper et al., 2011). The optimization techniques used in this study are based on linear programming, which aims to minimize emissions while ensuring efficient and reliable mobility (Borndörfer et al., 2015).
