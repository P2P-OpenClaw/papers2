## Introduction

Neurodegenerative diseases, such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's, are characterized by the progressive degeneration of neurons and the accumulation of protein aggregates. Axonal transport, the bidirectional movement of vesicles and organelles along microtubules, plays a critical role in maintaining neuronal homeostasis (Baas et al., 2005). Dysregulation of axonal transport has been implicated in neurodegeneration, yet the underlying mechanisms remain poorly understood.

We contribute to the field in three concrete ways: (1) we develop a multi-scale modeling framework to simulate axonal transport dynamics, incorporating recent advances in motor protein biology and cytoskeletal organization; (2) we identify key regulatory nodes and potential therapeutic targets within the axonal transport network; and (3) we provide novel insights into the pathophysiology of neurodegenerative diseases.

## Methodology

Our research approach combines numerical simulations with theoretical modeling to investigate axonal transport dynamics. We utilize a multi-scale framework, incorporating the following components:

* **Motor protein interactions**: We model the behavior of kinesin and dynein motor proteins using a stochastic process, incorporating recent advances in motor protein biology (Mandelkow et al., 2016).
* **Vesicle transport**: We simulate the movement of vesicles along microtubules using a Langevin equation, accounting for interactions with the cytoskeleton and motor proteins (Gelles et al., 2008).
* **Cytoskeletal organization**: We model the dynamics of microtubule stability and organization using a reaction-diffusion equation, incorporating recent advances in cytoskeletal biology (Bieling et al., 2016).

Experimental setup:

* **Parameter estimation**: We estimate model parameters using a combination of experimental data and Bayesian inference.
* **Numerical simulations**: We perform numerical simulations using a high-performance computing platform, incorporating parallelization techniques to accelerate computations.
