## Introduction

Topological quantum states are a class of materials that exhibit topological order, characterized by the presence of non-trivial edge states and robustness to defects. These states have been predicted to have unique properties, such as zero-energy modes, non-trivial Berry phases, and enhanced coherence times (Kitaev, 2001; Hasan & Kane, 2010). Theoretical models have proposed various approaches to create TQS, including the use of nanoscale engineering, quantum simulation, and machine learning algorithms (Baireuther et al., 2020; Zhang et al., 2020).

However, experimental realization of TQS has been challenging, mainly due to the difficulty in creating and manipulating the required topological structures. Current approaches rely on sophisticated techniques, such as nanoscale lithography, molecular beam epitaxy, and scanning tunneling microscopy (STM) (Hsieh et al., 2008; Wang et al., 2012). Despite these efforts, the quality and coherence of the created TQS are often compromised due to defects, impurities, and disorder.

In this study, we propose a novel approach to fabricate TQS using a combination of nanoscale engineering and quantum simulation. Our key technical insight is the utilization of a machine learning algorithm to optimize the fabrication process, leading to the creation of high-quality TQS with enhanced stability and coherence. We demonstrate the feasibility of our approach by fabricating a TQS using a 2D topological insulator, Bi2Se3, and measuring its topological invariant using a combination of STM and scanning tunneling spectroscopy (STS).
