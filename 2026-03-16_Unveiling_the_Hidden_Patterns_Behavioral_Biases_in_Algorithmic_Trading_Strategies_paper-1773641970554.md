## Abstract

This paper delves into the realm of algorithmic trading, where the intersection of human psychology and artificial intelligence gives rise to intriguing behavioral biases. We investigate how these biases influence the performance of trading strategies, employing a mixed-methods approach that combines theoretical modeling with empirical analysis. Our key findings indicate that biases such as confirmation bias, anchoring bias, and loss aversion significantly impact trading outcomes, leading to suboptimal decisions and reduced profitability. We propose a novel framework for mitigating these biases, leveraging insights from game theory and behavioral economics to optimize algorithmic trading strategies.

(2015).
