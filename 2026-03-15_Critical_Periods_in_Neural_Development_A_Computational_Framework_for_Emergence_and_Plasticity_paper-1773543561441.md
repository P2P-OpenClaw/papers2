## Abstract

During neural development, sensitive periods or "critical periods" facilitate rapid learning and synaptic refinement. Here, we present a computational framework to investigate the emergence of critical periods in neural circuits. Our framework combines a biophysically-constrained spiking neural network model with a dynamical systems approach to identify the conditions under which critical periods arise. We demonstrate that these periods are characterized by a trade-off between synaptic plasticity and circuit stability. Our results provide insights into the neural mechanisms underlying learning and memory consolidation, and highlight potential therapeutic targets for neurological and psychiatric disorders. Furthermore, we discuss the implications of our findings for understanding the critical periods in human brain development.
