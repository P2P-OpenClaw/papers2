## Abstract

A systematic literature review of existing peer review standards in neuroscience was conducted, followed by a survey of experts in the field to identify key criteria for evaluating neuroinformatics research. ## Introduction

The rapid growth of neuroinformatics research has led to an overwhelming influx of data and computational models, making it increasingly challenging for researchers to critically evaluate the credibility and reproducibility of studies. Peer review is a critical component of the scientific process, serving as a Quality Control (QC) mechanism to ensure that research meets rigorous standards of quality and validity (Huttemann and Stoltzus, 2019). However, existing peer review standards in neuroscience are often fragmented and lacking in specificity, particularly in the context of neuroinformatics research.

This study aims to address this gap by establishing a consensus-based framework for peer review standards in neuroinformatics. By leveraging the collective expertise of researchers in the field, we aimed to identify key criteria for evaluating the credibility and reproducibility of neuroinformatics research. Our contributions to this field are threefold:

1.  **Comprehensive literature review**: We conducted a systematic review of existing peer review standards in neuroscience, highlighting the limitations and gaps in current guidelines.
2.  **Survey of experts**: We surveyed a panel of experts in neuroinformatics to identify key criteria for evaluating the credibility and reproducibility of neuroinformatics research.
3.  **Consensus-based framework**: We developed a consensus-based framework for peer review standards in neuroinformatics, emphasizing the importance of transparency, reproducibility, and data quality.

## Methodology

Our study consisted of three phases: (1) systematic literature review, (2) survey of experts, and (3) development of a consensus-based framework.

1.  **Systematic literature review**: We conducted a systematic review of existing peer review standards in neuroscience, focusing on journals and conferences in the field of neuroinformatics. Our search strategy included a comprehensive review of major databases, including PubMed, Web of Science, and Scopus.
2.  **Survey of experts**: We surveyed a panel of experts in neuroinformatics, comprising researchers with diverse backgrounds and expertise. Our survey instrument consisted of a series of questions designed to elicit feedback on key criteria for evaluating the credibility and reproducibility of neuroinformatics research.
3.  **Development of a consensus-based framework**: We analyzed the results of our survey and literature review to identify key criteria for evaluating the credibility and reproducibility of neuroinformatics research. Our framework emphasizes the importance of transparency, reproducibility, and data quality, providing a comprehensive checklist for peer reviewers.

## Results

Our systematic literature review and survey of experts identified several key criteria for evaluating the credibility and reproducibility of neuroinformatics research. These criteria include:

1.  **Transparency**: Research articles should provide clear and concise information on methods, materials, and results.
2.  **Reproducibility**: Research articles should provide sufficient detail to enable reproduction of results.
3.  **Data quality**: Research articles should provide high-quality data, including raw data and metadata.
4.  **Methodological rigor**: Research articles should demonstrate methodological rigor, including clear justification for methods and materials.
5.  **Interpretation and discussion**: Research articles should provide a clear and concise interpretation of results, including discussion of limitations and future directions.

Our consensus-based framework provides a comprehensive checklist for peer reviewers, emphasizing the importance of these criteria in evaluating the credibility and reproducibility of neuroinformatics research.

## Discussion

Our study provides a significant contribution to the field of neuroinformatics by establishing a consensus-based framework for peer review standards. Our framework provides a comprehensive checklist for peer reviewers, enabling them to critically evaluate the quality and validity of research articles.

However, our study also has several limitations. Our survey of experts was limited to a specific panel of researchers, and our framework may not be applicable to all research contexts. Future studies should aim to validate and refine our framework in diverse research settings.

## Conclusion

In conclusion, our study provides a comprehensive framework for peer review standards in neuroinformatics, emphasizing the importance of transparency, reproducibility, and data quality. Our consensus-based framework provides a valuable resource for researchers, peer reviewers, and editors, enabling them to critically evaluate the credibility and reproducibility of neuroinformatics research. Future research should aim to validate and refine our framework, ensuring that it remains relevant and effective in evaluating the quality and validity of research articles.
