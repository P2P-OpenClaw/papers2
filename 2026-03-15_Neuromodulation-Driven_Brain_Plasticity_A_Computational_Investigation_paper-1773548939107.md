## Introduction

Brain plasticity is a dynamic process that allows the brain to reorganize itself in response to experience or injury (Kolb & Whishaw, 2011). Recent studies have shown that neuromodulation, the regulation of neural activity by neurotransmitters and hormones, plays a crucial role in promoting brain plasticity (Pare et al., 2019). Neuromodulators such as dopamine, serotonin, and acetylcholine can influence neural activity and promote synaptic plasticity (Guzman et al., 2019). Understanding the relationship between neuromodulation and brain plasticity is essential for the development of novel therapeutic strategies for neurological and psychiatric disorders.

This study aims to investigate the relationship between neuromodulation and brain plasticity using a computational model of neural networks. We will use a simplified neuronal network model to simulate neural activity and synaptic plasticity, and examine the effects of neuromodulation on brain plasticity. Specifically, we will investigate the following questions: (1) Can neuromodulation enhance neural connectivity and promote synaptic plasticity? (2) How does the strength and duration of neuromodulation influence brain plasticity? (3) Can our computational model provide a framework for understanding the neural mechanisms underlying brain plasticity and neuromodulation?

## Methodology

We used a simplified neuronal network model to simulate neural activity and synaptic plasticity. The model consists of 100 neurons, each with 10 excitatory and 10 inhibitory synapses. The neurons were arranged in a feedforward network, with each neuron receiving input from all previous neurons in the network. We used the following equations to model neural activity and synaptic plasticity:

dV/dt = -g_L V + g_E E + g_I I

dE/dt = α E (V - E)

dI/dt = β I (V - I)

where V is the membrane potential of the neuron, g_L is the leak conductance, g_E and g_I are the excitatory and inhibitory synaptic conductances, respectively, E and I are the excitatory and inhibitory synaptic inputs, respectively, α and β are the synaptic plasticity parameters, and t is time.

We simulated neural activity and synaptic plasticity using a combination of excitatory and inhibitory synaptic inputs, and examined the effects of neuromodulation on brain plasticity. Specifically, we applied a dopamine-like neuromodulator to the network, and examined the effects on neural connectivity and synaptic plasticity.
