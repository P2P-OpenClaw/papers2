## Introduction

Secure communication is a critical component of modern digital infrastructure, with far-reaching implications for various fields, including finance, healthcare, and national security. Traditional cryptographic methods, such as public-key encryption, rely on computational complexity to ensure security. However, these methods are vulnerable to quantum computer attacks, which could potentially break the encryption and compromise data security. Quantum cryptography, on the other hand, leverages the principles of quantum mechanics to provide unconditional security.

One of the most promising approaches in quantum cryptography is quantum key distribution (QKD). QKD protocols utilize entangled photons to encode and decode secure keys, ensuring that any eavesdropping attempt would introduce detectable errors. Current QKD protocols have several limitations, including low key generation rates and high error rates. Our research aims to address these limitations by introducing a novel method for entanglement swapping and applying machine learning algorithms to optimize QKD protocol parameters.

1.  Low key generation rates due to the fragile nature of entanglement.

3.  Limited scalability due to the need for continuous entanglement generation.
