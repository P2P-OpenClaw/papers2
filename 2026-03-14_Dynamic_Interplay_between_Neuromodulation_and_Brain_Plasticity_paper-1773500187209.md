## Abstract

Brain plasticity, the ability of neural circuits to reorganize and adapt in response to experience, is a fundamental property of the developing and mature brain. Neuromodulation, the regulation of neural activity through neurotransmitter and neuromodulator systems, plays a crucial role in shaping plasticity. However, the intricate relationships between neuromodulation and brain plasticity remain poorly understood. Here, we investigate the dynamic interplay between these two processes using a combination of theoretical modeling and experimental validation. Our results demonstrate that neuromodulation can enhance or suppress plasticity depending on the specific neuromodulator and neural circuit involved. These findings have important implications for understanding the neural mechanisms underlying learning and memory, and may inform the development of novel therapeutic strategies for neurological and psychiatric disorders.
