## Introduction

The advent of quantum computing and quantum information processing has sparked an increased interest in quantum internet architectures (QIAs). A QIA is a network of nodes capable of distributing entangled quantum states, enabling secure quantum key distribution (QKD) and quantum teleportation. In this work, we focus on designing a scalable QIA, leveraging the principles of entanglement swapping (ES) and quantum error correction (QEC).

Our contributions are threefold:

1.  **Scalable Entanglement Distribution**: We develop a decentralized network architecture, allowing for efficient entanglement distribution and sharing among multiple nodes.
2.  **Robust Quantum Communication**: Our framework ensures the security and reliability of quantum communication networks by incorporating QEC mechanisms and entanglement purification protocols.
3.  **Quantum Internet Performance Analysis**: We provide a rigorous mathematical analysis of the QIA's performance, including entanglement distribution rates, latency, and error correction capabilities.

Previous works on QIAs have focused on centralized architectures (Bennett et al., 1993; Bennett, 1992), which are limited in their scalability. Other approaches have employed quantum repeaters (Kimble, 2008) or quantum optical fibers (Huang et al., 2011), but these solutions come with significant technical and practical challenges.

## Methodology

Our QIA is based on a decentralized network of entanglement swapping nodes, connected through quantum channels. Each node is equipped with a quantum processor, enabling local quantum computations and entanglement manipulation. The architecture consists of three main components:

1.  **Entanglement Swapping Nodes**: These nodes are responsible for distributing entangled states among the network nodes. We employ a modified version of the ES protocol (Zukowski et al., 1993), allowing for efficient entanglement sharing.
2.  **Quantum Error Correction Mechanisms**: To ensure the reliability of quantum communication, we incorporate QEC mechanisms, such as quantum error correction codes (Gottesman, 1996) and entanglement purification protocols (Briegel et al., 1998).
3.  **Quantum Internet Performance Analysis**: We develop a theoretical framework to analyze the QIA's performance, including entanglement distribution rates, latency, and error correction capabilities. Our analysis is based on tools from quantum information theory, such as quantum channel capacities (Holevo, 1973) and entanglement measures (Werner, 1989).
