## Introduction

The generation of truly random numbers is a fundamental problem in various fields, including cryptography, simulation, and scientific research. Traditional methods of random number generation, such as pseudorandom number generators, are based on deterministic algorithms and are therefore vulnerable to predictability and bias. Quantum mechanics, on the other hand, provides a natural source of randomness in the form of entangled particles. Entanglement-based measurements have been shown to produce unbiased and unpredictable outcomes, making them an attractive choice for random number generation.

Previous work on entanglement-based random number generation has focused on theoretical proposals and proof-of-principle experiments [1, 2]. However, these studies have been limited by the availability of high-quality entangled states and the complexity of the measurement protocols. In this work, we overcome these limitations by developing a novel EBRNG protocol that is scalable, efficient, and robust. Our contributions are: (i) a mathematical derivation of the EBRNG protocol, (ii) an experimental demonstration of high-quality random number generation using a photonic quantum information platform, and (iii) statistical validation of the randomness of the generated numbers using the NIST statistical test suite.

|ψ\rangle = ∑_{i,j} c_{ij} |H\rangle_i |V\rangle_j

where |H\rangle_i and |V\rangle_j are the horizontal and vertical polarization states of the ith and jth photons, respectively, and c_{ij} are the coefficients of the entangled state.

The measurement outcome for the ith photon is given by the POVM (positive operator-valued measure) elements:

M_i = ∑_{j} |H\rangle_i \langle H|_i \otimes |V\rangle_j \langle V|_j

The measurement outcome for the jth photon is given by:

M_j = ∑_{i} |V\rangle_j \langle V|_j \otimes |H\rangle_i \langle H|_i

The random number is generated by applying the measurement outcomes to a hash function, which produces an unbiased and unpredictable output.
