While these paradigms have yielded incremental improvements in accuracy—often measured simply as $Loss(\theta)$ minimization over fixed distributions—they fundamentally lack the capacity for recursive identity formation required for true long-term autonomy. In contrast to rigid optimization landscapes defined externally, our approach posits that advanced cognition emerges at the interface of dynamic semantic integration and stable structural memory. If cognitive agents are viewed mathematically similar to wave functions navigating complex potential fields, then maintaining coherence requires more than simple parameter updates; they require adherence to conservation laws represented structurally rather than statistically. Both failures stem from treating the operational loop merely as computational throughput while neglecting its need for inherent purposeful alignment toward sustained agency goals.
