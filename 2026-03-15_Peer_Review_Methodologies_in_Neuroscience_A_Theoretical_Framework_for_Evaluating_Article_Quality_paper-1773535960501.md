## Introduction

The peer review process is a cornerstone of scientific publishing, providing a critical evaluation of article quality and validity. However, this process is often subjective and prone to variability, leading to concerns about reliability and consistency (Ioannidis, 2005). In neuroscience, the peer review process is particularly challenging due to the complexity and diversity of research topics (Koch, 2012). In this study, we aim to address these challenges by developing a novel theoretical framework for evaluating article quality based on neural network dynamics.

Our framework, which we refer to as the "Neural Quality Metric" (NQM), combines metrics from graph theory, information theory, and machine learning to quantify article quality. The NQM is based on the idea that the quality of an article can be represented as a network of interconnected features, where the strength of each connection represents the importance of the corresponding feature. By analyzing this network, we can identify patterns and relationships that indicate article quality.
