## Introduction

The manipulation of entanglement is a fundamental aspect of quantum information theory, with applications in quantum communication, computing, and cryptography. Entanglement swapping, a process by which entanglement is transferred from one pair of particles to another, has emerged as a crucial tool in quantum information processing. In this work, we explore the potential of entanglement swapping in enhancing the fidelity of quantum teleportation protocols.

Quantum teleportation, first proposed by Bennett et al. in 1993 [1], relies on the entanglement of two particles, often referred to as the "sender" and "receiver" particles. However, the fragile nature of entanglement renders it susceptible to decoherence and measurement-induced losses, compromising the fidelity of the teleportation process. Our research seeks to address this challenge by leveraging entanglement swapping to create a shared entanglement resource between the sender and receiver particles.
